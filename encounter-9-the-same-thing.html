Encounter 9 · The Same Thing?

What do you notice?

Two documents. 446 years apart. One is a journal entry by a European explorer claiming land for a Spanish monarch. The other is an administrative note by a US federal agency classifying a neighbourhood for mortgage risk.

They are not the same document. But they share something.

What do they share?
What is different about them?

Both documents describe people as passive objects to be assessed by an outside authority. Both produce a classification that determines what resources those people can access. Both claim a kind of neutral authority — the explorer speaks for the Crown and God; the assessor speaks for the federal government and the market.

Neither document acknowledges that it is making a choice. Both present themselves as describing what is already there.

The frame through which you see something determines what you see. The frame is never neutral. But the frame always claims to be.

What would it change — practically, not analytically — if these were the same thing?
What becomes invisible if we decide they are the same thing?
